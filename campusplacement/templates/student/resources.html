1. Numbers & LCM/HCF

Key Formulas:
• Product of two numbers = HCF × LCM
• LCM of fractions = LCM of numerators / HCF of denominators
• HCF of fractions = HCF of numerators / LCM of denominators
• If HCF(a, b) = 1, then a and b are co-prime
Q1: Find the HCF and LCM of 12 and 18.
A: 12 = 2² × 3, 18 = 2 × 3². HCF = 2 × 3 = 6. LCM = 2² × 3² = 36. Verify: 12 × 18 = 216 = 6 × 36 ✓
Q2: The HCF of two numbers is 12 and their LCM is 360. If one number is 60, find the other.
A: Other number = (HCF × LCM) / Given number = (12 × 360) / 60 = 72
Q3: Find the largest number that divides 615 and 963 leaving remainder 6 in each case.
A: Required number = HCF(615 − 6, 963 − 6) = HCF(609, 957) = 87

2. Percentages

Key Formulas:
• Percentage = (Value / Total) × 100
• % increase = [(New − Old) / Old] × 100
• % decrease = [(Old − New) / Old] × 100
• If A is x% more than B, then B is [x / (100 + x)] × 100% less than A
Q1: A number is increased by 20% and then decreased by 20%. Find the net change.
A: Net change = −(20²/100)% = −4% (decrease)
Q2: If the price of sugar rises by 25%, by what percentage should consumption be reduced to keep expenditure the same?
A: Reduction = [25 / (100 + 25)] × 100 = 20%
Q3: In an exam, 35% of students failed in Math, 25% failed in English, and 10% failed in both. What % passed in both?
A: Failed in at least one = 35 + 25 − 10 = 50%. Passed in both = 50%

3. Profit & Loss

Key Formulas:
• Profit % = [(SP − CP) / CP] × 100
• Loss % = [(CP − SP) / CP] × 100
• SP = CP × (100 + Profit%) / 100
• If two items sold at same SP, one at x% profit and other at x% loss: Net loss = x²/100 %
Q1: A shopkeeper buys an article for ₹500 and sells at ₹600. Find profit %.
A: Profit % = [(600 − 500) / 500] × 100 = 20%
Q2: By selling 12 apples, a vendor gains the SP of 4 apples. Find his profit %.
A: Profit on 12 = SP of 4 → CP of 12 = SP of 8. Profit % = (4/8) × 100 = 50%

4. Simple & Compound Interest

Key Formulas:
• SI = P × R × T / 100
• CI = P(1 + R/100)ⁿ − P
• Difference between CI and SI for 2 years = P × (R/100)²
• Amount doubles in T = 72/R years (Rule of 72)
Q1: Find SI on ₹5000 at 8% p.a. for 3 years.
A: SI = 5000 × 8 × 3 / 100 = ₹1200
Q2: The difference between CI and SI on a sum for 2 years at 10% is ₹50. Find the sum.
A: P × (10/100)² = 50 → P × 0.01 = 50 → P = ₹5000

5. Time, Speed & Distance

Key Formulas:
• Speed = Distance / Time
• 1 km/hr = 5/18 m/s; 1 m/s = 18/5 km/hr
• Average speed (same distance) = 2ab / (a + b)
• Relative speed (opposite direction) = a + b; (same direction) = |a − b|
Q1: A train 150m long passes a pole in 15 seconds. Find its speed in km/hr.
A: Speed = 150/15 = 10 m/s = 10 × 18/5 = 36 km/hr
Q2: A person covers half the distance at 40 km/hr and the other half at 60 km/hr. Find average speed.
A: Avg speed = 2 × 40 × 60 / (40 + 60) = 4800/100 = 48 km/hr

6. Time & Work

Key Formulas:
• If A does work in 'a' days → A's 1 day work = 1/a
• A and B together: 1/a + 1/b = (a+b)/ab → Time = ab/(a+b)
• If A is twice as efficient as B, A takes half the time of B
Q1: A can do a job in 10 days, B in 15 days. Together, how many days?
A: Together = (10 × 15) / (10 + 15) = 150/25 = 6 days
Q2: A can do a piece of work in 20 days. He works for 5 days and B finishes the remaining in 10 days. In how many days can B alone do the whole work?
A: A does 5/20 = 1/4 in 5 days. Remaining = 3/4. B does 3/4 in 10 days → Full work = 10 × 4/3 = 13⅓ days

7. Ratio & Proportion

Key Formulas:
• a : b = c : d → ad = bc (cross multiplication)
• Componendo: (a+b)/b = (c+d)/d
• Dividendo: (a−b)/b = (c−d)/d
• If A:B = 2:3 and B:C = 4:5, then A:B:C = 8:12:15
Q1: Divide ₹1200 among A, B, C in ratio 2:3:5.
A: A = 1200 × 2/10 = ₹240, B = ₹360, C = ₹600
Q2: If 2A = 3B = 4C, find A:B:C.
A: Let 2A = 3B = 4C = k → A = k/2, B = k/3, C = k/4 → A:B:C = 6:4:3

8. Averages

Key Formulas:
• Average = Sum of observations / Number of observations
• If avg of n₁ items is a₁ and n₂ items is a₂: Combined avg = (n₁a₁ + n₂a₂) / (n₁ + n₂)
• Average of first n natural numbers = (n+1)/2
Q1: The average of 5 numbers is 20. If one number is excluded, the average becomes 18. Find the excluded number.
A: Sum = 5 × 20 = 100. New sum = 4 × 18 = 72. Excluded = 100 − 72 = 28
Q2: The average age of 30 students is 15. When the teacher's age is included, the average increases by 1. Find the teacher's age.
A: New avg = 16, Total = 31 × 16 = 496. Students' sum = 30 × 15 = 450. Teacher's age = 496 − 450 = 46

1. Blood Relations & Direction Sense

Major Concepts: Family tree construction, identifying relations based on a statement.
Minor Concepts: Directional tracking, Pythagorean theorem for shortest distance.
Q1: Pointing to a photograph, a man said, "She is the only daughter of my wife's mother-in-law". How is the woman related to the man?
A: The man's wife's mother-in-law is the man's mother. The only daughter of his mother is his sister.
Q2: A man walks 5km South, turns right and walks 3km. Then he turns left and walks 5km. In which direction is he from the starting point?
A: He went South, then West, then South again. So he is in the South-West direction.

2. Number Series & Coding-Decoding

Major Concepts: Arithmetic progressions, squares/cubes series, alphabetical shifting (Caesar cipher).
Minor Concepts: Alternate series, prime number sequences.
Q1: Find the next number in the series: 2, 6, 12, 20, 30, ...
A: The differences are 4, 6, 8, 10. Next difference is 12, so 30 + 12 = 42.
Q2: If 'APPLE' is coded as 'EQQPI', how is 'MANGO' coded?
A: Each letter is shifted forward. A(+4)=E, P(+1)=Q, P(+1)=Q, L(+4)=P, E(+4)=I. For MANGO, the logic might simply be shifting vowels and consonants differently, but if we shift all by +4: M(+4)=Q, A(+4)=E, N(+4)=R, G(+4)=K, O(+4)=S. Correct code: QERKS.

1. Arrays & Strings

Major Concepts: Two Pointers, Sliding Window, Prefix Sum, Maximum Subarray (Kadane's Algorithm).
Minor Concepts: Hashing for frequency counting, slow and fast pointers.
Q1: How do you find the missing number in an array of 1 to N?
A: Use the sum of first N natural numbers formula: `N * (N + 1) / 2`. Subtract calculating sum of array elements from this to get the missing number. Alternatively, use XOR logic.
Q2: What is the time complexity of reversing an array in place?
A: O(N), where we swap elements from both ends using two pointers moving towards the center. Space complexity is O(1).

2. Trees & Graphs

Major Concepts: Tree Traversals (Inorder, Preorder, Postorder), BFS and DFS in Graphs, Shortest Path (Dijkstra's).
Minor Concepts: Topological sorting, Minimum Spanning Tree (Kruskal/Prim), Cycle detection.
Q1: What is the difference between a Binary Tree and a Binary Search Tree (BST)?
A: A Binary Tree can have its nodes arranged in any order. In a BST, the left child is always lesser than the parent, and the right child is always greater.
Q2: Explain BFS vs DFS in terms of Data Structures used.
A: BFS uses a Queue (FIFO) to explore nodes level by level. DFS uses a Stack (LIFO) or recursion to explore as deep as possible before backtracking.

1. Database Management Systems (DBMS)

Major Concepts: ACID Properties, Normalization (1NF, 2NF, 3NF, BCNF), SQL Joins, Indexing.
Minor Concepts: Transactions, views, triggers, NoSQL vs SQL.
Q1: What are the ACID properties?
A: Atomicity (all or nothing), Consistency (valid state before and after), Isolation (concurrent execution equals serial execution), Durability (committed changes survive failures).
Q2: Differentiate between Primary Key and Unique Key.
A: Both enforce uniqueness. A Primary Key cannot have NULL values, and only one primary key exists per table. A Unique Key can accept one NULL value, and a table can have multiple unique keys.

2. Operating Systems (OS) & Networks

Major Concepts: Process & Thread, Deadlocks, Virtual Memory, OSI Model, TCP vs UDP.
Minor Concepts: Paging, Mutex vs Semaphore, IP Addressing, HTTP vs HTTPS.
Q1: What is a deadlock and what are its necessary conditions?
A: Deadlock is a situation where a set of processes are blocked because each process is holding a resource and waiting for another resource acquired by some other process. Conditions: Mutual Exclusion, Hold and Wait, No Preemption, Circular Wait.
Q2: TCP vs UDP?
A: TCP is connection-oriented, reliable, and ensures data ordering (e.g., web browsing). UDP is connectionless, faster, but less reliable with no guarantee of delivery (e.g., live video streaming).

1. PHP Fundamentals

Major Concepts: Variables, Loops, Arrays, Superglobals.
Minor Concepts: Sessions, Cookies, File Handling.
Q1: What is a Superglobal in PHP?
A: Built-in variables that are always available in all scopes. Examples: $_GET, $_POST, $_SERVER, $_SESSION.
Q2: Difference between require() and include()?
A: Both include a file, but require() produces a FATAL ERROR and stops the script if the file is not found, whereas include() only produces a WARNING and the script continues.

2. PHP with Databases (MySQL)

Major Concepts: PDO vs MySQLi, Prepared Statements, CRUD operations.
Minor Concepts: SQL Injection prevention.
Q1: How do you prevent SQL Injection in PHP?
A: Use Prepared Statements with PDO or MySQLi. This ensures that parameters are not executed as SQL commands.

1. Machine Learning Basics

Major Concepts: Supervised vs Unsupervised Learning, Regression vs Classification.
Minor Concepts: Overfitting, Underfitting, Bias-Variance Tradeoff.
Q1: What is Supervised Learning?
A: Training a model using labeled data where the input and the correct output are provided. Example: Predicting house prices based on features.
Q2: How do you handle Overfitting?
A: Use techniques like Cross-Validation, Regularization (L1/L2), Pruning (in Trees), or simply getting more training data.

2. Data Science Libraries (Python)

Major Concepts: Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-Learn.
Minor Concepts: Matplotlib, Seaborn.
Q1: What is a Pandas DataFrame?
A: A two-dimensional, size-mutable, potentially heterogeneous tabular data structure with labeled axes (rows and columns).

1. Search Engine Optimization (SEO)

Major Concepts: On-Page SEO, Off-Page SEO, Keyword Research.
Minor Concepts: Backlinks, Meta Tags, Page Load Speed.
Q1: What is the difference between On-Page and Off-Page SEO?
A: On-Page refers to optimizations on your own website (content, keywords, tags). Off-Page refers to actions outside your site impacting your rankings (backlinks, social signals).
Q2: What are Long-Tail Keywords?
A: Highly specific search phrases that usually have lower search volume but much higher conversion rates because they target specific intents.

2. PPC & Analytics

Major Concepts: Pay-Per-Click Ads, Conversion Rate, CTR, Bounce Rate.
Minor Concepts: ROI, A/B Testing.
Q1: What is CTR and how is it calculated?
A: Click-Through Rate. It is calculated as (Total Clicks / Total Impressions) * 100.

1. Personal/Behavioral Questions

Major Concepts: Introduction structure, STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for behavioral questions.
Minor Concepts: Body language cues, tone of voice, handling tricky/stress tests.
Q1: Tell me about yourself.
A: Break it down into Present, Past, and Future. Talk about your current role/degree, highlight 1-2 major achievements or skills, and explain why you're a great fit for the role you're interviewing for. Keep it concise.
Q2: Describe a time you failed and how you handled it.
A: Be honest but strategic. Use the STAR method. Emphasize what you learned from the failure and the steps you took to make sure it didn't happen again. Avoid citing critical flaws.

2. Career & Company Fit

Major Concepts: Aligning company values with personal goals, explaining career shifts, discussing salary expectations.
Minor Concepts: Asking insightful questions at the end of the interview.
Q1: Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
A: Focus on professional growth. Mention you want to master your current role, take on leadership or mentorship responsibilities eventually, and contribute significantly to the company's long-term goals.
Q2: Why do you want to work for our company?
A: Show that you've done your research. Mention specific products, recent news, or workplace culture elements that appeal to you. Tie those back to your own skills and what you can uniquely contribute.
